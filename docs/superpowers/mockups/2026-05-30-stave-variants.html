Say It Right — pick a prosody stave style

Same sentence in 4 rendering styles. Tell me the number you want (mix-and-match notes welcome).

Sentence: “Turn it off and on again.”  ·  ● stressed / ●●  nuclear (the focus word) · · reduced · ↗ ↘ thought-group tone · ‿ connected speech (liaison) · ‖ thought-group break

Style 1

Enhanced marks (Raycast-style, but colored & interactive)

The familiar dots-above-words layout from the Raycast version, upgraded with color, a colored/underlined nuclear word, and a real tone arrow per group. Closest to what you already know; lowest risk.

●Turn‿·it‿●off ↗‖ ·and●on‿●again↘
/ˈtɜrn ɪt ˈɔf ‖ ən ˈɑn əˈɡɛn/

Best for: familiarity, dense info, easy to scan word-by-word.

Style 2

Pitch contour (the one Raycast literally cannot draw)

An actual intonation curve drawn over the words — you see the melody rise and fall. Dots sit on the line at stressed syllables (big = nuclear). This is the strongest “VS Code > Raycast” signature.

Turnitoff andonagain
/ˈtɜrn ɪt ˈɔf ‖ ən ˈɑn əˈɡɛn/

Best for: hearing-the-melody learners; pairs beautifully with the karaoke word-highlight in a later phase (the dot can ride the curve as it plays).

Style 3

Height-mapped stave (words sit at their pitch)

Words are placed vertically by pitch/stress — stressed words ride high, reduced words sink, the nuclear word is boxed. Faint staff lines give a “musical notation” feel; spacing hints at rhythm.

Turn it off and on again
/ˈtɜrn ɪt ˈɔf ‖ ən ˈɑn əˈɡɛn/

Best for: a spatial, intuitive sense of stress & melody at a glance; very “unlike a text editor.”

Style 4

Typographic + under-curve (clean reading flow)

No marks above the line. Stress is shown by size and weight, reduced words are dimmed and small, the nuclear word is colored. A small curved underline under each group shows the tone. Reads like prose, scales to long passages.

Turn it‿ off and on‿ again
/ˈtɜrn ɪt ˈɔf ‖ ən ˈɑn əˈɡɛn/

Best for: minimal & modern; best for multi-sentence passages where dots-above would get noisy.
